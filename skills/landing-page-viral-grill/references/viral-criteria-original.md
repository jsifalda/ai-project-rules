# Viral checklist

Takeaways from Marc Lou's "32 Principles of a Viral Product", restated in brief.
Source: https://x.com/marclou/status/2065385672991752210 — read the original for the full argument.

32 checks across 7 sections. The auditing skill reads this file and scores a target
against every bullet below, in file order, adding nothing.

Check IDs are derived by position, not written here: section initial + index within
the section (Pricing bullet 1 = `PR1`, Headline bullet 2 = `HE2`). Reordering or
inserting a bullet renumbers everything after it and invalidates past reports, so
append within a section rather than inserting.

## Pricing

- No free plan. <3% convert. Free users = support load + server cost + wrong roadmap.
- Hard paywall. Signups aren't validation. Payment before data.
- Popcorn pricing: 3 tiers max. Good / Better / Best. Extra tier = extra exit.
- "Pricing" in the header. People read it to understand the product, not just cost.
- No subscription unless unavoidable. One-time = 10x easier sell.
- Price *above* competitors. "Nobody talks about the second cheapest option."

## Headline

- Fifth-grader test. Complexity kills curiosity.
- Numbers > adjectives. "Fast" → "Save 4 hours every week".
- Emotional. Laugh, wow, or WTF. People remember feelings, not features.
- Next-day test: write 5, show friends, ask 24h later which stuck.
- <10 words. Can't say it in one sentence → users won't either.

## Copy

- Only you could write it. Competitor could paste it onto their site → too generic.
- Steal from customers. They describe it better than you.
- No weak words ("most", "many", "rarely"). Claims people can picture + challenge.
- Sell the human desire, not the feature → money, time, health, status, less pain.
- Empathy first. Describe the problem better than they can.

## Page structure

- Sell from the hero alone. 80% never scroll. Fix hero first.
- One idea per screen. Instagram-feed logic.
- One CTA. Multiple paths → many pick none.
- CTA names the action. "Get Started" → "Analyze My Website".
- Show, don't tell. Demo > paragraphs.
- Play before pay. Best features *on* the landing page, not behind the wall.

## Proof & framing

- Shareable footer. 97% won't buy, might share. Last thing seen = remembered.
- Comparison table. They care *why switch*, not what you do.
- Testimonials before launch. Proof before traffic. Beta testers count.

## Design

- Three colors. Black text, white bg, one accent for Buy. Every color competes.
- OG image = YouTube thumbnail. Seen more than the site. No click, no watch.
- Memorable name. Known words. No wordplay, no invented words.

## Positioning

- Do one thing. Nobody remembers a Swiss Army knife.
- Ride a wave. Existing trend does half the marketing.
- Do something unseen. Nobody shares a clone.
- Visible founder. Face + screen recording > corporate promo.
